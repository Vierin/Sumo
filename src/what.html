What is SUMO?

Musculoskeletal Diagnosis and Treatment can be a Complex Process

Diagnosing the disorders and health conditions associated with the musculoskeletal system may not seem as simple to diagnose. Multiple musculoskeletal disorders exist that cause various symptoms stemming from various factors within the patient.

Imaging tests such as MRIs or CT scans will need to be performed and upon diagnosis can treatment plans go into effect. X-rays are typically performed first but there is difficulty viewing soft tissue damage using this method to determine a diagnosis. Soft tissue damage can be difficult to diagnose as a musculoskeletal disorder in the early stage. This can be due to the lack of past health knowledge of patients, the difficulty in tracking necessary health statistics of patients, and operations proved to be insufficient and time-consuming.

The common denominator regarding inadequate diagnoses and treatment in musculoskeletal care is data issues. With data breaking new limits in capacity in healthcare, it is vital that appropriate systems are in place to not only withstand big data collection but also promote ease in analysation. SUMO is the future of patient data in musculoskeletal care; breaking barriers that many healthcare professionals found difficult to manage.
